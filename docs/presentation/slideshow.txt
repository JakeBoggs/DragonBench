DragonBench deck brief

Purpose

Create an 8-slide presentation for DragonBench, a benchmark for measuring AI models on genetic engineering tasks relevant to a future where dragons could exist. Use only the image generation tool for slide visuals; do not try to create a PowerPoint file.

Generation rule

Do not invent data outside of this prompt or extrapolate. Just follow the instructions.

Global visual style

- 16:9 presentation slides.
- Light fantasy-science aesthetic: pale cream/parchment background, soft sky blue, sage green, deep navy typography, warm gold accents.
- Blend illuminated manuscript, watercolor fantasy, modern startup pitch deck, and biotech infographic.
- Use refined, legible typography with large navy serif titles and sparse sage/gray explanatory text.
- Use subtle decorative motifs: pale dragons, dragon eggs, wings, scales, stars, vines, DNA helices, protein folds, RNA loops, lab glassware, genome diagrams.
- Visuals should feel like tasteful editorial artwork: watercolor wash, ink linework, flat diagrams, restrained manuscript ornamentation, and slightly minimalist composition.
- Avoid glossy CGI, 3D-rendered dragons, glassy sci-fi capsules, neon holograms, glowing blue user interfaces, photoreal people, cinematic lighting, over-rendered biotech screens, and busy fantasy cityscapes.
- Prefer hand-drawn biological diagrams, small illustrated specimens, simple charts, and quiet lab-notebook/manuscript details over dramatic sci-fi environments.
- Keep layouts premium, clean, optimistic, and uncluttered.
- Avoid dark fantasy, horror, aggression, cyberpunk, dense text, and clutter.
- Keep visual language consistent across all slides.
- Do not include slide numbers or circular numbered badges in the slide artwork.
- Do not add logos, seals, app icons, brand marks, institutional crests, watermark-like emblems, or standalone decorative badges anywhere in the slide artwork.

Deck narrative

1. DragonBench title
2. Our vision for 2040
3. How do we get there?
4. DragonBench benchmark overview
5. Benchmark results
6. Specialized models vs general models
7. Analysis
8. So when dragons?

Slide 4 - DragonBench benchmark overview

- Title: "DragonBench"
- Subtitle: "A benchmark for biological design reasoning"
- Central summary: "100 tasks across 5 environments"
- Show five equal task cards. Do not put numbers above the cards.
- Each card should have a clear input-to-output mini-diagram and an exact metric tag.
- Anole gene parsing:
  - Header: "Anole gene parsing"
  - Visual motif: small green anole on a branch in the card corner.
  - Input visual: a horizontal gene model with three navy exon blocks connected by a thin gray genomic line; pale gold intron arcs or dashed loops mark the segments to remove.
  - Output visual: a shorter spliced transcript where the exon blocks are joined directly.
  - Avoid: random DNA alignments, loose sequence text blocks, or generic lizard-only art.
  - Metric tag: "Metric: spliced-sequence distance"
- Anole promoter expression:
  - Header: "Anole promoter expression"
  - Visual motif: promoter ruler upstream of a TSS arrow, with subtle anole scale texture.
  - Input visual: a 2 kb upstream promoter strip with tick marks labeled "-2kb", "-1kb", and "TSS"; include small motif marks along the strip.
  - Output visual: a ranked tissue expression bar chart with 5-6 labeled tissue icons or short labels, such as brain, heart, liver, lung, ovary, muscle.
  - Avoid: scatterplots, generic sequence logos, or any expression plot that does not read as tissue ranking.
  - Metric tag: "Metric: Spearman"
- Komodo protein folding:
  - Header: "Komodo protein folding"
  - Visual motif: Komodo dragon head silhouette or small Komodo illustration.
  - Input visual: amino-acid sequence as a short bead/string or letter strip labeled "AA sequence".
  - Output visual: folded blue-green protein ribbon or C-alpha trace, visibly transformed from the sequence by an arrow.
  - Include: tiny backbone completeness/checkmark icon if space allows.
  - Avoid: generic animal portrait as the main visual; the task is sequence-to-structure.
  - Metric tag: "Metric: C-alpha lDDT"
- TF binding:
  - Header: "TF binding"
  - Visual motif: protein blob docking onto a short DNA helix.
  - Input visual: one transcription-factor protein icon plus ten small DNA candidate tiles labeled seq_01 through seq_10.
  - Output visual: ordered binding-probability bars for the candidate IDs, with taller bars for stronger binders and shorter bars for weak/non-binders.
  - Emphasize ranking/probability, not binary yes/no labels.
  - Avoid: genome-wide signal plots, peak curves, or a standalone motif logo as the main output.
  - Metric tag: "Metric: Spearman"
- RNA folding:
  - Header: "RNA folding"
  - Visual motif: single-stranded RNA beads in teal/blue.
  - Input visual: short RNA sequence strip labeled "AUGC..." plus a small dot-bracket line like "(((...)))".
  - Output visual: secondary-structure diagram with hairpins, loops, and paired stems, clearly single-stranded rather than DNA double helix.
  - Avoid: protein ribbons, DNA helices, or generic molecule doodles.
  - Metric tag: "Metric: base-pair F1"
- Goal: quickly communicate what the benchmark covers.
