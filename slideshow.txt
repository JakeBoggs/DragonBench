DragonBench Visual Prompt Pack

Global style brief

We are creating a fantasy-science pitch deck for a project called DragonBench, a benchmark for measuring AI models on genetic engineering tasks relevant to a future where dragons could exist.

The deck should feel ambitious, whimsical, premium, and scientific. Visual style: light-colored fantasy illustration, not dark fantasy. Use a pale cream or parchment background, soft sky-blue, sage green, navy typography, and warm gold accents. The aesthetic should blend illuminated manuscript, watercolor fantasy, modern startup pitch deck, and elegant biotech infographic.

Design motifs:

Pale dragons, dragon eggs, wings, scales, stars, celestial symbols.

DNA helices, protein folds, genome diagrams, RNA loops, lab glassware.

Thin gold borders, art-nouveau corner ornaments, subtle vines/leaves.

Clean slide layout with strong hierarchy and lots of whitespace.

Typography should feel refined and legible: large elegant serif titles in deep navy, smaller explanatory text in sage green or muted gray.

Use light, airy, optimistic illustrations. Avoid dark backgrounds, horror, aggression, cyberpunk, or clutter.

The slides should look consistent as one deck.

Important text-rendering instruction:Keep text sparse and large. Do not overfill slides with small labels. Any charts or tables should be clean, approximate, and visually polished. Exact numbers can be placeholders.

Deck narrative:

DragonBench title.

Our vision for 2040.

How do we get there?

The bottleneck: biological design reasoning / DragonBench benchmark.

Benchmark results.

Specialized models vs general models.

What the results tell us.

When dragons?

Slide 7 prompt: What the results tell us

Create Slide 7 of the same presentation deck. Match the light fantasy-science aesthetic: parchment background, ornate gold border, pale dragon illustration in the corner, refined analytical layout, navy and sage typography.

The slide title should read:

“What the results tell us”

Subtitle:

“General models excel at ranking and sequence tasks, but struggle on structure — and still lag behind expert biological systems.”

Design this as an analysis slide with four insight cards. Each card should have a small supporting mini-chart or icon.

Insight cards:

“Sequence reasoning is improving”Supporting visual: small DNA icon and mini bar chart.

“Structure remains hard”Supporting visual: geometric protein/fold icon and mini bar chart.

“Specialized models still lead”Supporting visual: trophy or laurel icon and mini comparison chart.

“There’s a meaningful gap to experts”Supporting visual: target icon and small gap chart.

The overall message should be that general models perform better on ranking and sequence-based tasks than on geometry-heavy structure prediction, and there is still a meaningful gap to expert biological systems.

Use lots of whitespace. Keep it clean, analytical, and beautiful. Add refined fantasy-science illustrations in the corners, such as a pale dragon, telescope, books, flask, compass, DNA emblem, or vines.
